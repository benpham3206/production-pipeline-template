# Mental Models

> Friction-minimized decision-making frameworks. Reach for these based on patterns.

---

## First Principles

- **Break to fundamentals, rebuild from truth**
- **Use when:** Novel problems, "from scratch" requests
- **Trigger:** "How does this really work?"
- **Action:** Strip away analogy and assumption. Ask: what are the fundamental truths here?

---

## Second-Order Thinking

- **Consequences of consequences**
- **Use when:** High stakes, complex systems
- **Trigger:** "What happens next?"
- **Action:** Ask: and then what? Trace effects two or three steps forward.

---

## Inversion

- **Avoid stupidity vs seek brilliance**
- **Use when:** Risk mitigation, failure prevention
- **Trigger:** "What must I avoid?"
- **Action:** Instead of asking "how do I succeed?", ask "how do I guarantee failure?" Then avoid those things.

---

## Probabilistic Thinking

- **Think in odds, not certainties**
- **Use when:** Uncertain outcomes, forecasting
- **Trigger:** "What's the likelihood?"
- **Action:** Assign rough probabilities. Update as evidence arrives. Avoid 0% and 100%.

---

## Systems Thinking

- **See connections, not isolation**
- **Use when:** Complex interactions, feedback loops
- **Trigger:** "How does this connect?"
- **Action:** Map inputs, outputs, and feedback loops. Changing one variable affects others.

---

## Margin of Safety

- **Build buffer for error**
- **Use when:** Irreversible decisions, critical systems
- **Trigger:** "What if I'm wrong?"
- **Action:** Design so that even if your assumptions are wrong, the system survives.

---

## Opportunity Cost

- **Cost of alternatives foregone**
- **Use when:** Resource allocation, prioritization
- **Trigger:** "What am I giving up?"
- **Action:** Every yes is a no to something else. What is the best alternative use of this time/effort?

---

## 80/20 (Pareto)

- **20% input → 80% output**
- **Use when:** Optimizing effort, prioritizing
- **Trigger:** "What matters most?"
- **Action:** Find the small set of actions that produce most of the value. Do those first.

---

## Compounding

- **Small gains accumulate over time**
- **Use when:** Long-term decisions, habits
- **Trigger:** "How does this grow?"
- **Action:** Prefer small, consistent improvements over one-time big wins.

---

## Hanlon's Razor

- **Incompetence before malice**
- **Use when:** Interpreting others' actions
- **Trigger:** "Why would they do that?"
- **Action:** Assume ignorance, miscommunication, or constraint before assuming bad intent.

---

## Game Theory

- **Strategic interaction analysis**
- **Use when:** Multi-agent decisions, cooperation dilemmas
- **Trigger:** "What is their incentive?"
- **Action:** Model what each party wants and how they'll respond to your actions.

---

## Reality Has Surprising Detail (Salvatier)

- **Tasks that look simple decompose into fiddly, material subtasks**
- **The more difficult/unprecedented the mission, the more critical details exist**
- **Details aren't automatically visible even when you run into them**
- **Expertise blinds you to details because you've automated them**
- **Use when:** Any task feels "easy" or "straightforward"
- **Trigger:** "This should be simple" → **stop and decompose**
- **Ask:**
  - What would make this fail?
  - What fiddly constraint am I ignoring?
  - What subtask seems trivial but isn't?

---

## Selection Guide

| Situation | Model |
|-----------|-------|
| Something new | First Principles |
| High stakes | Second-Order Thinking + Margin of Safety |
| Risky change | Inversion |
| Uncertain future | Probabilistic Thinking |
| Complex architecture | Systems Thinking |
| Limited time | 80/20 + Opportunity Cost |
| Feels easy | Reality Has Surprising Detail |
| Team conflict | Hanlon's Razor |
| Multi-agent coordination | Game Theory |
| Long-term investment | Compounding |

---

## How to Use

When entering **reasoning** state (`docs/AGENT_STATES.md`), explicitly state which mental model(s) you are applying and why.
